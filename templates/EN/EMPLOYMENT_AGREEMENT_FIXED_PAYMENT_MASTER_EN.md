**Employer**

**Employee**
- Full legal name: **[COMPANY NAME]**
- Address: **[ADDRESS]**
- CPR/ID No.: **[CVR]**

The Employer and the Employee are each a "Party" and together the "Parties".

## 2. Position and Duties

### 2.1 Position

The Employee is employed as **[JOB TITLE]**.

### 2.2 Responsibilities

The Employee shall perform duties associated with the position and any other reasonable tasks assigned by the Employer consistent with the Employee’s qualifications.

### 2.3 Loyalty and Conduct

The Employee shall act loyally and in the best interests of the Employer and comply with internal policies and applicable laws.

## 3. Commencement and Duration

### 3.1 Start Date

Employment commences on **[DATE]**.

### 3.2 Type of Employment

The employment is **[TERM TYPE] until [DATE]**.

### 3.3 Probation Period (If Applicable)

The first **[NUMBER] months** shall constitute a probation period, during which termination may occur with **[NUMBER] [TIME UNIT]**, subject to applicable law.

## 4. Working Hours

### 4.1 Normal Hours

The Employee’s normal working hours are **[NUMBER]** hours per week, typically scheduled between **[WORKING SCHEDULE]**.

### 4.2 Additional Work

The Employee may be required to perform reasonable additional work as necessary for business needs. Overtime compensation applies only if expressly agreed or required by mandatory law.

## 5. Remuneration

### 5.1 Salary

The Employee shall receive a fixed gross salary of **[NUMBER] [CURRENCY]** per month.

### 5.2 Payment

Salary shall be paid monthly in arrears to the Employee’s designated bank account.

### 5.3 Variable Compensation (If Applicable)

Any bonus, commission, or incentive scheme shall be governed by separate written terms and shall not form part of the guaranteed salary unless expressly stated.

### 5.4 Pension and Benefits (If Applicable)

The Employer shall provide pension contributions and other benefits as specified in Schedule 1 or in accordance with company policy.

## 6. Holidays and Leave

### 6.1 Holiday Entitlement

The Employee is entitled to holiday and holiday pay in accordance with applicable law.

### 6.2 Statutory Leave

The Employee is entitled to statutory leave, including sickness, parental leave, and other protected leave, subject to applicable law.

### 6.3 Illness Reporting

Absence due to illness must be reported without undue delay in accordance with company procedures.

## 7. Confidentiality

### 7.1 Confidential Information

The Employee shall not disclose confidential information concerning the Employer’s business, clients, suppliers, or employees.

### 7.2 Survival

This obligation continues after termination of employment without limitation in time.

## 8. Intellectual Property

### 8.1 Ownership

All work products, inventions, materials, and intellectual property created by the Employee in the course of employment shall belong exclusively to the Employer to the extent permitted by law.

### 8.2 Assignment

The Employee assigns all transferable rights to such intellectual property to the Employer without additional compensation unless otherwise required by law.

## 9. Non-Competition and Non-Solicitation (If Applicable)

### 9.1 Separate Terms

Any non-compete or non-solicitation obligation must comply with mandatory employment legislation and shall be separately specified in Schedule 2, if applicable.

### 9.2 Statutory Compensation

If compensation is required by law for post-employment restrictions, such compensation shall be paid in accordance with statutory requirements.

## 10. Secondary Employment

The Employee may not undertake other employment or business activities that conflict with the Employer’s interests without prior written consent.

## 11. Company Property

All equipment, documents, and materials provided by the Employer remain the Employer’s property and shall be returned upon request or termination.

## 12. Data Protection

The Employer processes the Employee’s personal data in accordance with applicable data protection legislation and internal privacy policies.

## 13. Termination

### 13.1 Notice Period

Either Party may terminate the employment by giving written notice in accordance with applicable law or as follows:
- Employer notice: **[NUMBER] months**
- Employee notice: **[NUMBER] months**

### 13.2 Immediate Termination

The Employer may terminate the employment without notice in case of gross misconduct or material breach, subject to applicable law.

### 13.3 Garden Leave (If Applicable)

The Employer may require the Employee not to perform duties during the notice period while continuing salary payment.

## 14. Liability

The Employee is liable for losses caused by intentional misconduct or gross negligence, subject to applicable law.

## 15. Assignment

The Employee may not assign rights or obligations under this Agreement. The Employer may transfer this Agreement as part of a business transfer, merger, or restructuring.

## 16. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of Denmark. Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of **[LOCATION]**, unless mandatory law provides otherwise.

## 17. Language

This Agreement is executed in English, which shall prevail in case of inconsistency.

## 18. Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties and supersedes prior agreements or understandings relating to the employment. Any amendment must be in writing and signed by both Parties.

## 19. Execution

This Agreement may be executed in counterparts and by electronic signature, each of which shall be deemed an original.
