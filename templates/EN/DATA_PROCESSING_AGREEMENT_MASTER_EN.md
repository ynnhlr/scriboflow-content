The Controller and the Processor are each a "Party" and together the "Parties".

## 2. Purpose and Scope

### 2.1 Processing Relationship

This Agreement governs the Processor’s processing of Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.

### 2.2 Main Agreement

This Agreement forms part of and supplements the underlying service agreement between the Parties (the "Main Agreement").

### 2.3 Conflict

In the event of conflict between this Agreement and the Main Agreement, this Agreement prevails regarding data protection matters.

## 3. Definitions

For the purposes of this Agreement:
- "Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.
- "Processing" means any operation performed on Personal Data.
- "Data Subject" means the individual to whom Personal Data relates.
- "Applicable Data Protection Law" means Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR) and any applicable national data protection legislation.

## 4. Nature and Purpose of Processing

### 4.1 Purpose

The Processor shall process Personal Data solely for the purpose of performing services under the Main Agreement.

### 4.2 Data Subjects and Data Types

The categories of Data Subjects and types of Personal Data processed are described in Schedule 1.

### 4.3 Duration

The duration of processing shall correspond to the duration of the Main Agreement unless otherwise required by law.

## 5. Instructions

### 5.1 Controller Instructions

The Processor shall process Personal Data only on documented instructions from the Controller.

### 5.2 Unlawful Instructions

If the Processor believes that an instruction infringes Applicable Data Protection Law, the Processor shall promptly inform the Controller.

## 6. Confidentiality

The Processor shall ensure that persons authorised to process Personal Data are bound by confidentiality obligations or statutory confidentiality duties.

## 7. Security Measures

### 7.1 Appropriate Measures

The Processor shall implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk.

### 7.2 Measures Include

Where appropriate, measures include:
- access controls
- encryption or pseudonymisation
- regular testing of security controls
- incident response procedures

### 7.3 Documentation

The Processor shall document implemented security measures and provide them upon reasonable request.

## 8. Sub-Processors

### 8.1 Prior Authorisation

The Processor shall not engage sub-processors without prior written authorisation from the Controller.

### 8.2 General Authorisation

Where general authorisation is granted, the Processor shall inform the Controller of intended changes and allow objection within **[Number]** days.

### 8.3 Flow-Down Obligations

The Processor shall impose data protection obligations on sub-processors equivalent to those set out in this Agreement.

### 8.4 Liability

The Processor remains fully liable for the performance of sub-processors.

## 9. International Transfers

### 9.1 Consent

The Processor shall not transfer Personal Data outside the EU/EEA without prior written consent from the Controller.

### 9.2 Safeguards

Any such transfer shall be subject to appropriate safeguards under Applicable Data Protection Law, including:
- Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
- adequacy decisions
- other lawful transfer mechanisms

## 10. Assistance to the Controller

### 10.1 Data Subject Requests

The Processor shall assist the Controller in responding to Data Subject requests.

### 10.2 Compliance Assistance

The Processor shall assist the Controller in ensuring compliance with obligations relating to:
- security of processing
- data protection impact assessments
- prior consultation with supervisory authorities

### 10.3 Compensation

Assistance may be subject to reasonable compensation where legally permissible.

## 11. Personal Data Breach

### 11.1 Notification

The Processor shall notify the Controller without undue delay and no later than **[Number]** hours after becoming aware of a Personal Data breach.

### 11.2 Contents

The notification shall include:
- description of the breach
- categories and approximate number of affected Data Subjects
- likely consequences
- measures taken or proposed

## 12. Audit and Compliance

### 12.1 Information

The Processor shall make available information necessary to demonstrate compliance.

### 12.2 Audits

The Controller may conduct audits upon reasonable notice, no more than **[Number]**, unless required by law or following a security incident.

## 13. Return or Deletion of Data

### 13.1 Return or Delete

Upon termination of the Main Agreement, the Processor shall, at the Controller’s choice, return or delete all Personal Data.

### 13.2 Legal Retention

The Processor may retain data only where required by law.

## 14. Liability

### 14.1 Statutory Liability

Each Party shall be liable in accordance with Applicable Data Protection Law.

### 14.2 Processor Indemnity

The Processor shall indemnify the Controller for losses resulting from breach of this Agreement or unlawful processing.

### 14.3 Liability Caps

Any limitation of liability in the Main Agreement shall not apply to breaches of data protection obligations unless explicitly stated.

## 15. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of Denmark. Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of **[Location]**, unless mandatory law provides otherwise.

## 16. Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the complete understanding between the Parties regarding data processing and supersedes prior arrangements relating to such subject matter.

## 17. Execution

This Agreement may be executed in counterparts and by electronic signature, each of which shall be deemed an original.
