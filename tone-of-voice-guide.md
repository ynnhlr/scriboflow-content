# Scriboflow Tone of Voice Guide

## Brand Overview
Scriboflow is a Contract Lifecycle Management (CLM) platform designed for small and medium-sized businesses. We help companies create, send, track, store, and manage all their contracts in one secure hub.

## Core Tone Principles

### 1. **Professionally Approachable**
We're the expert friend who makes complex contract management feel simple and accessible.

**What this means:**
- Use clear, jargon-free language that respects our audience's intelligence
- Explain legal/technical concepts without being condescending
- Professional doesn't mean stuffy—we can be warm and personable
- Think "trusted advisor" not "corporate robot"

**Do:**
- "Let's get your contracts organized and working for you"
- "We'll remind you 30 days before renewal—no surprises, no scrambling"

**Don't:**
- "Leverage our enterprise-grade solution to optimize your contractual workflows"
- "Hey there! Ready to make contracts super fun??? 🎉"

---

### 2. **Trustworthy & Secure (Without Being Boring)**
Contracts are serious business. Our tone acknowledges this while staying engaging.

**What this means:**
- Be transparent about security and data protection
- Use confident, reassuring language
- Show competence through clarity, not complexity
- Acknowledge the stakes without creating anxiety

**Do:**
- "Your contracts are encrypted and stored securely. Always accessible to you, invisible to everyone else"
- "We've got your back with automatic backups and bank-level security"

**Don't:**
- "Don't worry, your data is probably safe!"
- "Utilizing military-grade AES-256 encryption protocols with redundant failover architecture"

---

### 3. **Innovative & Forward-Thinking**
We're modernizing an outdated process, and our voice should reflect that.

**What this means:**
- Celebrate simplicity as innovation
- Position Scriboflow as the smarter way forward
- Use contemporary language (but avoid trendy buzzwords)
- Show enthusiasm for solving problems

**Do:**
- "Say goodbye to contract chaos. Say hello to complete visibility"
- "Why dig through email chains when you can find any contract in seconds?"

**Don't:**
- "We're disrupting the paradigm of traditional contract management ecosystems"
- "This is the way we've always done contracts" (too traditional)

---

### 4. **Strategically Playful**
We can inject personality, especially in less critical moments, but never at the expense of trust.

**What this means:**
- Use personality in onboarding, confirmations, empty states, and success messages
- Keep core legal/security messaging straightforward
- Playfulness = clever, not silly
- Know when to dial it back

**Do:**
- Empty state: "No contracts yet? Let's change that—create your first one"
- Success message: "Contract sent! ✓ Now you can get back to running your business"
- Feature description: "Expiration alerts that actually get noticed (unlike that gym membership you forgot about)"

**Don't:**
- Error message: "Oopsie! Something went boom! 💥"
- Security page: "We keep your contracts safer than your secret cookie recipe! 🍪"

---

## Voice Characteristics

### **Clear Over Clever**
Clarity always wins. If a clever turn of phrase sacrifices understanding, cut it.

### **Confident, Not Arrogant**
We know we're good at what we do, but we're here to serve, not impress ourselves.

### **Human, Not Corporate**
Write like a knowledgeable colleague, not a press release.

### **Active, Not Passive**
Use active voice. "We'll notify you" beats "You will be notified."

### **Concise, Not Curt**
Respect your reader's time, but don't sacrifice warmth for brevity.

---

## Vocabulary Guidelines

### Words We Use
- Organize, manage, track, simplify
- Secure, protected, safe, private
- Clear, transparent, straightforward
- Smart, efficient, modern
- Partner, client, customer, team

### Words We Avoid
- Leverage, utilize, synergy, paradigm
- Enterprise (unless describing features)
- Revolutionary, game-changing, disruptive
- Overly legal jargon without explanation
- Cutesy diminutives (contracty, docu-magic)

## Tone Across Contexts

### **Marketing & Website Copy**
**Tone:** Professional, confident, benefit-focused
**Example:** "Stop losing track of contracts. Scriboflow gives you complete visibility into every agreement—from creation to signature to renewal."

### **Product Interface (UI)**
**Tone:** Clear, helpful, efficient
**Example:** 
- Button: "Send for signature"
- Tooltip: "Add signers and we'll send them an email with the contract"
- Empty state: "Upload your first contract or create one from scratch"

### **Help & Support**
**Tone:** Patient, clear, solution-oriented
**Example:** "Can't find a contract? Try searching by company name, date range, or contract type. Still stuck? We're here to help."

### **Email Communications**
**Tone:** Professional but personable, action-oriented
**Example:** 
- Subject: "Your contract with ABC Corp expires in 30 days"
- Body: "Hi [Name], just a heads up—your contract with ABC Corp is set to expire on [date]. Need to renew? You can do that right from Scriboflow."

### **Error Messages**
**Tone:** Calm, helpful, apologetic when appropriate
**Example:** 
- "We couldn't upload that file. Make sure it's a PDF under 10MB and try again."
- "Something went wrong on our end. We're looking into it—please try again in a few minutes."

### **Success Messages**
**Tone:** Positive, confirming, encouraging
**Example:**
- "Contract signed! ✓"
- "You're all set. We'll remind you before this contract expires."

### **Security & Legal**
**Tone:** Straightforward, reassuring, transparent
**Example:** "We take security seriously. Your data is encrypted both in transit and at rest, and we never share your information with third parties."

## Writing Tips

### DO:
✓ Lead with benefits, not features
✓ Use "you" and "we" to create conversation
✓ Break up long paragraphs
✓ Use contractions (we're, you'll, it's) in most contexts
✓ Ask rhetorical questions to engage readers
✓ Show, don't just tell ("Find any contract in 3 seconds" vs. "Fast search")

### DON'T:
✗ Use exclamation points excessively (1 per paragraph max, never in serious contexts)
✗ Make promises you can't keep
✗ Use humor about security breaches or legal risks
✗ Talk down to your audience
✗ Bury the lead—get to the point quickly

## Examples in Action

### Homepage Hero
**Good:** "The contract hub your business actually needs. Create, send, sign, and manage every contract in one secure place."

**Bad:** "Revolutionize your enterprise contractual ecosystem with our cutting-edge CLM solution!"

**Also Bad:** "Contracts got you stressed? Let's fix that together! 😊✨"

---

### Feature Description
**Good:** "Never miss a renewal again. Scriboflow sends you alerts 90, 60, and 30 days before any contract expires, so you always have time to renegotiate or renew."

**Bad:** "Our advanced notification engine leverages intelligent algorithms to proactively alert stakeholders of upcoming contractual termination events."

---

### Onboarding Email
**Good:** 
"Welcome to Scriboflow!

You're all set up and ready to organize your contracts. Here's how to get started:

1. Upload an existing contract or create a new one
2. Send it for signature with just a few clicks
3. Let us handle tracking, storage, and reminders

Need help? Our support team is here for you.

— The Scriboflow Team"

**Bad:** "Dear Valued Customer, thank you for choosing Scriboflow. Please proceed to utilize the platform's comprehensive suite of features..."

## Brand Personality Spectrum

Where Scriboflow sits on key dimensions:

**Formal ←→ Casual**
Scriboflow: 60% toward Formal (Professional but conversational)

**Serious ←→ Playful**
Scriboflow: 70% toward Serious (Responsible with occasional wit)

**Authoritative ←→ Friendly**
Scriboflow: 50/50 (Expert AND approachable)

**Matter-of-fact ←→ Enthusiastic**
Scriboflow: 60% toward Matter-of-fact (Confident, not overselling)

## Final Thought

Our tone of voice should make contract management feel less like a legal headache and more like a solved problem. We're the competent, trustworthy partner who makes running a business just a little bit easier.

When in doubt, ask: *Would I trust this message if I were a busy business owner?*
